Challenges —

The complexity of internal flows.

Intralogistics has become a strategic pillar for modern industry. The growing complexity of product ranges, shorter production cycles, and the constant need for full traceability put traditional storage and handling systems under pressure. Warehouses and factories are expected to move materials faster, safer, and more intelligently — all while operating in spaces designed for people and subject to continuous change.

This is where robotics and artificial intelligence can reshape the paradigm: by enabling autonomous, collaborative systems that integrate seamlessly into human-centric environments. Humanoid robots, equipped with cognitive intelligence, are able to support operators in tasks that combine precision, strength, and adaptability, making internal flows more resilient and sustainable.

Challenge Humanoid & AI-enabled solution
Increasing product variety & smaller batches Adaptive handling and dynamic route planning
Need for full traceability Cognitive vision and data integration with WMS/MES
Safety & ergonomics Collaborative robots operating in shared environments
Operational efficiency Autonomous navigation and predictive optimization

Oversonic has developed RoBee Series F to embody this evolution: a humanoid robot that brings intelligence, strength, and adaptability into intralogistics.

From bottleneck management to human-centered safety, cognitive robotics transforms intralogistics from a cost factor into a strategic advantage.

RoBee F: humanoid intelligence for logistics flows.

Within this landscape, RoBee F represents the model specifically designed by Oversonic for intralogistics. It combines humanoid dexterity with high-payload handling, an autonomous omnidirectional base, and a modular rear transport unit. RoBee F supports the management of pallets, containers, and industrial components across multiple levels and workstations, automating internal flows while interacting safely with operators. Unlike traditional AGVs or conveyor systems, it adapts dynamically to changing routes and demands, ensuring both efficiency and human-centered collaboration.
