RoBee in manufacturing

Industrial operations are facing structural challenges: workforce shortages, increasing safety requirements, and the unsustainability of repetitive and strenuous tasks. Productivity alone is no longer enough — companies must ensure resilience, safety, and well-being for their workers. RoBee responds to these needs by acting as a cognitive and collaborative resource: a machine that learns, adapts, and shares the workspace with people, enabling a sustainable balance between efficiency and human value.

Compliancy —

RoBee is a certified machine.

A humanoid robot for industrial use must meet rigorous certification requirements. Unlike traditional automation systems, RoBee shares workspaces with people, moves autonomously, and handles sensitive data. Each of these dimensions requires compliance with rigorous international standards.

RoBee meets the CE marking and the requirements of the Machine Directive 2006/42/CE, providing assurance that it can function as an industrial-grade machine within the European regulatory framework. Compliance with EMC–EMI standards ensures that its electronic systems neither generate nor suffer from electromagnetic interference, a key requirement in complex production facilities.

Equally important are the protections for data and information security. With ISO 27001:2013 certification and GDPR compliance, RoBee guarantees that interaction and data exchange remain secure, protecting both industrial processes and personal information. On the operational side, the robot is tested to IPX4 protection rating, making it resilient to real-world use conditions.

The name —

RoBee takes its name from the bee, a symbol of tireless work and collective intelligence. Like bees in a hive, RoBee is designed to operate with efficiency and precision as part of a network — collaborating with humans and with other robots.
